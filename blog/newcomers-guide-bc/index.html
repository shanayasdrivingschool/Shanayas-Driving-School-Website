New to BC? Driving Rules Newcomers Should Know

New to BC? The road rules that catch newcomers off guard: right turns on red, 30 km/h school zones, winter tires, distracted driving, and licensing.

By Shanaya's Driving School · Updated February 10, 2026 · 7 min read · Newcomer Resources

If you've recently moved to British Columbia, understanding the local driving rules and licensing process is one of the first things to take care of. Here's a clear guide to help you get started. For the full step-by-step licensing walkthrough, see our BC driver's licence guide for newcomers.

Step 1: Visit an ICBC Driver Licensing Office

Within 90 days of becoming a BC resident, you must obtain a BC driver's licence. Bring your current licence, two pieces of ID, and proof of residency.

Step 2: Understand the Graduated Licensing Program

BC uses a graduated licensing system (GLP). Depending on your existing experience, you may need to complete the Learner (L) and Novice (N) stages before receiving a full licence. See our BC Graduated Licensing Program guide for the full stage-by-stage breakdown.

Step 3: Learn BC-Specific Rules

  • Right turns on red are allowed (unless posted otherwise)
  • School zones are 30 km/h during posted hours
  • Playground zones are 30 km/h dawn to dusk
  • Distracted driving laws are strictly enforced
  • Winter tires are required on many highways from October to April

Step 4: Consider Professional Lessons

Even experienced international drivers benefit from professional lessons to understand local road culture, signage differences, and defensive driving techniques used in BC.

"Learning the local rules is the fastest way to feel confident on BC roads."
